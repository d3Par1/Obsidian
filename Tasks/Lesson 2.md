Petcube is a smart device that was created in Kyiv in 2012 by three Ukrainian friends. They had a
dog that often felt lonely when they were at work, so they decided to design something new.
The first Petcube allowed owners to watch their pets through a small camera and even play with
them using a built-in laser pointer. Later versions have included a microphone and a speaker, so
people can talk to their pets, and some models can even throw treats remotely.
Petcube has quickly grown from a small start-up into an international company. Today, its
products have been sold in more than 18 countries and are especially popular in the United States.
Many pet owners say it has made life easier and more fun, both for them and for their animals.
Petcube has also helped to show the world that Ukraine is a centre of creative and useful
technology.

Grammarly is a writing assistant that was created in 2009 by three Ukrainians: Alex Shevchenko, Max
Lytvyn and Dmytro Lider. At first, the software helped students check their essays and avoid mistakes.
Later, it became popular with professionals who needed to write clear emails and reports. Grammarly
can correct grammar, spelling and vocabulary errors, but it also suggests how to improve style and
tone. The app works as a website, a browser extension and a mobile app, so people can use it almost
anywhere. Today, Grammarly has more than 30 million daily users and has become one of the 
best-known Ukrainian tech products worldwide.
